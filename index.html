Clement Attlee

"There's a lot less there than meets the eye."

Clement Richard Attlee was a British statesman and Labour Party politician who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1945 to 1951. It is said that when Attlee went to see King George at Buckingham Palace to be appointed Prime Minister, the notoriously laconic Attlee and the famously tongue-tied King stood in silence; Attlee finally volunteered the remark, "I've won the election." The King replied "I know. I heard it on the Six O'Clock News".

During this time his government achieved the following:

  • The Leader of the Labour Party from 1935 to 1955.
  • Undertook the nationalisation of public utilities and major industries.
  • Created the National Health Service.
  • Attlee is rated by scholars, critics and the public as one of the greatest British Prime Ministers.
  • National Insurance Act 1946
